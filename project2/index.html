FOR the Proposition

Design Decisions and Rationale:

AGAINST the Proposition

Design Decisions and Rationale:

  • -1.5: Normalized by complainant group to downplay total complaint volume and shift interpretation.
  • -1: Removed annotations so that reader cant compare values exactly
  • -1: Framed title in a way that implies equality and doesnt emphasize dominance by one ethnic group

Final Reflection

Designing both persuasive and oppositional visualizations from the same dataset was harder than I thought it would be. The first visualization, which was for the proposition was easy to create after doing some exploratory data analysis. The design decisions were like any other visualizations I create and there were barely any deceptive methods used hence showing the reality of the data making it ethcial.

Generating the opposing visualization was the tricky part. To be able to counter the proposition I had to transform the data by column normalization to construe the real meaning of the data. I had to make sure my deceptions were not obvious while also being persuasive at the same time. It was a slightly more technical process but not too hard which made me realize how easy it is to create visualizations to lead masses the wrong way.

I've defined ethical analysis as showing visualizations from the true data without having to transform it into making readers think otherwise. Ive realized that to make a visualization persuasive we do not need to conform to unethical means to transform our existing data to tell a fake story. In my second visualization I crossed the boundary of ethics to change key content to mislead readers. I've learnt that the transparency of design decision are key for a viewer to understand the process of creating the visualiztion and for them to accept that the persuasion is not unethical.
